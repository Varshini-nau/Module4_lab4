Addendum

In converting my resume to HTML, I made several structural choices to ensure the content was clearly organized and easy to read. Here are the key decisions and reasons behind them:

  • Font Choices: I chose Arial for its readability and professional look. It's a clean, sans-serif font that works well for both headings and body text.
  • Sizes of Headings and Main Text: The main text is set to 1rem for readability, with headings incrementally larger to create a clear hierarchy. H1 is 2em, H2 is 1.5em, and H3 is 1.2em.
  • Color Choices: I used a blue (#0056b3) for headings to create a professional and calming appearance. The background is a light gray (#f4f4f9) to reduce eye strain.
  • Margin and Padding Choices: The body has no margin or padding to utilize the full viewport space. Sections and headers have margins to create space between content, and padding within sections enhances readability.

HTML Abuse

I corrected any misuse of HTML tags. For example, I ensured that the highest-level headings used <h1> tags and structured subheadings with <h2> and <h3> tags appropriately.
